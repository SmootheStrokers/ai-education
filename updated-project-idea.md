Company Overview
Mission: Make AI literacy accessible, practical, and trustworthy for non-technical Americans.

Brand Position: "AI explained for everyday people."

Target Audience: Small business owners, contractors, realtors, service professionals, and older professionals (ages 35-65+) with low AI literacy who know AI matters but don't know where to start.

Core Problem: The AI education market is dominated by technical content built for developers and tech workers. Millions of working Americans are being left behind — not because they can't benefit from AI, but because nobody is explaining it in their language, for their workflows.

Market Opportunity
The gap is real and growing. Pew Research Center has found that a majority of Americans have heard of AI tools like ChatGPT but that comfort and usage vary significantly by age, education, and industry. McKinsey's research on AI adoption shows that small businesses lag significantly behind enterprises. The U.S. Small Business Administration counts over 33 million small businesses in America — the vast majority without dedicated technology staff.

This creates a massive underserved market: people who run businesses, manage teams, and serve clients, but who have no guide to help them use AI in practical, immediate ways.

This is the operational advantage that makes a small team competitive with much larger education companies.

How Claude Code and agentic AI power the business:
[3/10/2026 2:27 AM] Jarredynnn Rizzzdartz: Research automation: Agents pull and synthesize findings from industry reports, government data (SBA, BLS), and reputable publications. A human editor verifies, curates, and adds context — but the research legwork that would take hours takes minutes.

Every factual claim must have a source. If it can't be sourced, it's framed as opinion or emerging trend, clearly labeled.
Distinguish between "proven" and "promising." Use language like "early evidence suggests" vs. "research confirms" appropriately.
No hype, no fear-mongering. The brand doesn't say "AI will replace you" or "AI will solve everything." It says "here's what's real, here's what's useful, here's how to start."
Corrections policy: When errors are identified, they are corrected publicly and promptly. This builds trust faster than pretending to be perfect.
Date-stamp all recommendations. AI tools change fast. Every guide and playbook includes a "last verified" date so users know how current the information is.

Trust-Building Tactics for Skeptical Non-Tech Audiences
This audience doesn't trust Silicon Valley. They trust people who talk like them, show real results, and don't condescend. Every tactic must reflect this.

Lead with people, not technology. Every piece of content starts with a person, a problem, or a story — never with jargon or product specs.

Use their language. Not "leverage AI to optimize your workflow." Instead: "Use this free tool to write your emails in half the time."

Show, don't tell. Screen recordings of actually using the tools. Before-and-after comparisons. "Here's what I typed, here's what it gave me, here's how I used it."

Feature people who look like them. Case studies and testimonials from contractors, realtors, and small business owners — not tech founders. Real faces, real names, real results.
[3/10/2026 2:27 AM] Jarredynnn Rizzzdartz: Acknowledge the skepticism directly. Content like "I was skeptical about AI too — here's what changed my mind" or "5 things people get wrong about AI (and what's actually true)."

Start with free. The newsletter, YouTube content, and Facebook Group are all free. Build trust before asking for money. The audience needs to see value multiple times before they'll buy.

No manipulation. No fake scarcity, no countdown timers on evergreen products, no "only 3 spots left" on unlimited digital products. This audience has been burned by internet marketing tactics. Be straightforward.

Local presence. Speaking at local business events, partnering with chambers of commerce, appearing on local podcasts. This demographic trusts local more than national.

Satisfaction guarantees. 30-day money-back guarantee on all products. Reduces risk for a price-sensitive audience.

Consistent delivery. The newsletter goes out every week. The YouTube video goes up every week. Consistency builds trust with this audience more than anything else.
